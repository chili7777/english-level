📋 There is/are & Perfect Tenses Reference

▼
🔹 Existencia: "There is / There are" en Todos los Tiempos
Tense / Structure Singular Plural When to Use
Present There is a problem.
Hay un problema.		 There are two problems.
Hay dos problemas.		 Use for things that exist NOW. ✅
Past There was a problem.
Había un problema.		 There were two problems.
Había dos problemas.		 For past existence. ⏮️
Future There will be a problem.
Habrá un problema.		 There will be two problems.
Habrá dos problemas.		 For future existence. ⏭️
Present Perfect There has been a problem.
Ha habido un problema.		 There have been problems.
Ha habido problemas.		 For experiences or past situations connected to now. 🔗
Past Perfect There had been a problem.
Había habido un problema.		 There had been problems.
Había habido problemas.		 For something that existed before another past event. ⏮️⏮️
Future Perfect There will have been a problem.
Habrá habido un problema.		 There will have been problems.
Habrá habido problemas.		 Rare, but used for completed existence in the future. ⏭️✅
Would Have There would have been a problem.
Habría habido un problema.		 There would have been problems.
Habría habido problemas.		 Third conditional situations. 🤔
Should Have There should have been a problem.
Debería haber habido un problema.		 There should have been problems.
Debería haber habido problemas.		 Regret or criticism. 😔
Could Have There could have been a problem.
Podría haber habido un problema.		 There could have been problems.
Podría haber habido problemas.		 Possibility in the past. 🤷
Might Have There might have been a problem.
Podría haber habido un problema.		 There might have been problems.
Podría haber habido problemas.		 Probability in the past. 🎲
✅ 1️⃣ PRESENT PERFECT
Uso: Acción pasada con conexión al presente.
Pronombre Inglés Español
I I have worked Yo he trabajado
You You have worked Tú has trabajado
He He has worked Él ha trabajado
She She has worked Ella ha trabajado
It It has worked Eso ha trabajado
We We have worked Nosotros hemos trabajado
They They have worked Ellos han trabajado
Existencia:
• There has been a problem (singular)
• There have been problems (plural)
= Ha habido
